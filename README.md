# Data-Analytics-Projects

**Project Overview**
This project is an interactive Power BI dashboard that analyzes credit card defaulter behavior using a real-world dataset. The goal is to identify patterns and trends in credit card defaults across key demographics including education level, gender, age group, and marital status, and to provide actionable recommendations for the financial sector.


**Dataset**  
**Source:** Kaggle - Default of Credit Card Clients Dataset  
**Records:** 30,000 customers  
**Features:** Demographics, credit limit, payment history, bill amounts (6 months)  
**Used for**: Practice and educational purposes only  


**Findings**
**Age Group:** The 30-40 age group carries the highest default volume (4.1bn), making them the most financially vulnerable segment  
**Education:** University-educated customers had the highest defaulter count (~3K), showing that education alone does not prevent financial risk  
**Gender:** Female customers showed a slightly higher defaulter count (~4K) vs Male (~3K)  
**Marital Status:** Single and Married individuals had nearly equal default rates (~3K each), suggesting marital status is not a strong differentiator  
**Credit Limit:** Non-Defaulters hold a significantly higher average credit limit (0.18M) vs Defaulters (0.13M)  
**Billing Trends:** Monthly bill amounts remained stable (1.2bn to 1.5bn range), suggesting systemic and ongoing repayment challenges  


**Financial Sector Recommendations**
**Based on the dashboard insights, the following actions are recommended:**
Implement risk-based credit limits targeting the 30-40 age group  
Introduce early warning systems and proactive outreach for customers showing repayment stress  
Leverage predictive analytics and ML models to flag high-risk profiles before defaults occur  
Design targeted financial literacy programs since even educated customers are defaulting  
Reassess gender-neutral credit policies and explore behavioral patterns beyond demographics  
Monitor monthly billing trends continuously as persistently high bill amounts are early indicators of future defaults
